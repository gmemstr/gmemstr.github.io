1.1. Gabriel

1.1.1. Basic Coffee

James Hoffman's AeroPress technique

  • 11g of coffee
  • 200g of hot water
  • Boiled, but not bubbling in the kettle

Brew for 2 minutes, swirl gently, sit for 30 more seconds then press down.

  1. Experiment: Boiling Water

    Coffee: Taylors of Harrogate Decaffe Ground

    What?: Seeing how the taste is affected by using freshly boiled water, rather than waiting for it to cool for a minute until the bubbles disapate.

    Result: A layer of something on top, and of course I have to wait longer for it to cool down. Drinking without milk (because I ran out) or sugar (because sugar). Tastes a little too acidic off the bat, although that seems to have subsided a touch as it cools. Too cool and it becomes too bitter. I think this isn't a good method and the water temp needs to be lower than ~90 (roughly estimating how much the water cools when it stops bubbling).

1.1.2. Inverted Method

  • 11g of coffee
  • 50g + 150g hot water

Best with freshly ground coffee. Invert AeroPress with plunger at or near (1) and add coffee. Bloom coffee for ~30 seconds, then add rest of water and let brew for ~2 minutes. Pre-wet filter paper, attach filter and then place mug or glass on top. Hold plunger and main chamber with one hand, mug with the other, and flip. Press firmly until completely drained. Can technically flip the AeroPress without the mug on top but is slightly riskier.
